All nouns in Pashto are either <Masc /> or <Fem />. Thankfully, you can pretty much always tell the gender of a word by its ending.

## Gender by Ending

**<span style={{ backgroundColor: mascColor }}>Masculine</span> words end in:**

 - a consonant
 - <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "ÛŒ", f: "ey" }} />
 - <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "Ù€Ù‡", f: "u" }} />
 - <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "ÙˆÛŒ", f: "ooy" }} /> or <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "Ø§ÛŒ", f: "aay" }} />

**<span style={{ backgroundColor: femColor }}>Feminine</span> words end in:**

 - all the other vowels (<InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "Ø§", f: "aa" }} />, <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "Ù€Ù‡", f: "a" }} />, <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "ÙŠ", f: "ee" }} />, <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "Û", f: "uy" }} />, etc.)

**A couple of other things to watch out for:**

- Words ending in <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{p:"Ùˆ", f:"oo"}} /> can be either <Masc /> or <Fem />.
- Words ending in <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "Ù€Ù‡", f: "u" }} /> can also be <Fem /> plural, as in <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "Ø§ÙˆØ¨Ù‡", f: "oobu", e: "water" }} />

## Exceptions

### Feminine words that lost their <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "Ù€Ù‡", f: "a" }} />

Some <Fem /> words have had their <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{p:"Ù€Ù‡", f:"a"}} />'s on the end chopped off. This makes them look like masculine nouns, but actually they do behave (and get inflected) just like other feminine nouns. For example, the word <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{p:"ÙˆØ±Ú", f:"wradz", e:"day"}} /> ends in a consanant, but it is <Fem />, and it <Link to="/inflection/inflection-intro">inflects</Link> just like any other feminine word ending with an <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{p:"Ù€Ù‡", f:"a"}} />, ie. <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{p:"ÙˆØ±ÚÛ", f:"wradze", e: "days - plural" }} />.

Here are some other <Fem /> words that have lost their <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{p:"Ù€Ù‡", f:"a"}} /> ending:

export function IrregularFem() {
    return <ul>
        {femEndingWConsonant.map(({ entry }) => (
            <li key={entry.ts}>
                <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: entry.p, f: firstVariation(entry.f), e: firstVariation(entry.e) }} />

    </ul>;
}

For instance for the word "road/way" you will hear people saying both <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "Ù„Ø§Ø±", f: "laar" }} /> and <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "Ù„Ø§Ø±Ù‡", f: "laara" }} />.
