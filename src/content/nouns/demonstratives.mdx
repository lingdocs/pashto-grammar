Demonstratives are a special kind of <Link to="/nouns/determiners/">determiner words</Link> that are used **to point to or specify nouns**. These are are words that work like "this, that, these, those." In Pashto we use the words for demonstratives:

- <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "ÿØÿß", f: "daa", e: "this / these" }} />
- <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "ÿØÿ∫Ÿá", f: "d√°gha", e: "this / these" }} />
- <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "Ÿáÿ∫Ÿá", f: "h√°gha", e: "that / those" }} />

And these words also inflect in some interesting ways. TLDR; See the <Link to="/pronouns/demonstratives/#overview--tables">tables at the bottom</Link>.

## The general ÿØÿß - daa

The word <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "ÿØÿß", f: "daa", e: "this / these" }} /> is probably the most common demonstrative in Pashto. It's also fairly general, for pointing to people or things close by or at a medium distance. ("Distance" here can refer to the distance in conversation, not just physical distance.)

The other nice thing about this useful pointer word is that **it is the same for masculine or feminine, singular or plural**!

### Inflecting ÿØÿß - daa to ÿØ€ê - de

In all these examples above, masculine and feminine, singular or plural, we always use <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "ÿØÿß", f: "daa" }} />. But what happens when we need to <Link to="/inflection/inflection-intro/">inflect</Link> a word because it's <Link to="/sandwiches/sandwiches/">in a sandwich</Link> or because it's <Link to="/verbs/past-verbs/#past-tense-with-transitive-verbs-">the subject of a past tense transitive verb</Link>? In that case it becomes <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "ÿØ€ê", f: "de" }} />.

Remember, <Link to="/inflection/inflection-intro/">normally</Link> we would inflect a word when it's plural, but not with <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "ÿØÿß", f: "daa" }} />! **we only inflect this word for _two_ reasons**:

- being in a sandwich
- being the subject of a past tense transitive verb

Here are some examples of the demonstrative <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "ÿØÿß", f: "daa" }} /> being inflected because it's used for the subject of a past tense transitive verb.
