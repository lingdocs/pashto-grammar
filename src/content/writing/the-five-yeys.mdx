## Some Important Notes

#### The Û vs. Ø¦ confusion

Because the <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "Û", f: "uy" }} /> and <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "Ø¦", f: "eyy" }} /> sound so similar (and in some dialects may be identical in pronunciation) people often confuse them in writing. To avoid this confustion it's important to remember this *very simple rule*: âš–

 - <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "Ø¦", f: "eyy" }} /> is only used for 2nd person plural verb endings
 - <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "Û", f: "uy" }} /> is only used at the end of feminine nouns and adjectives

When you write a ÛŒ or a ÙŠ, they both appear as a Ù€ÛŒÙ€ in the middle of words. So when you see a Ù€ÛŒÙ€ it can be either:

##### Pakistani with ÙŠ

This is used by the [Pashto Academy of Peshawer University](http://khyber.org/pacademy/#) and in many current publications. The only big difference from standard Afghan spelling is that for the "ey" sound the Urdu Û’ letter is used instead of ÛŒ. 

##### Pakistani with ÛŒ 

This is used in [Speaking Pakistani Pukhto](https://www.speakingpashto.com) and in much written material in Pakistan. This system differs more from standard Afghan spelling because in addition to the Urdu Û’ being used, the ÛŒ is used for "ee" instead.

##### Old Pakistani

This system was used in older writings and only uses three ÛŒ letters (Û’ØŒ ÛŒØŒ Ø¦) which are used for different sounds.
