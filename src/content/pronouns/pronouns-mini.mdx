## Where they're used

For Pashto learners, it can be confusing to see these little pronouns peppered in sentences. They can seem ambiguous and confusing, as if they could mean *anything*. Thankfully Pashto gives us another very simple rule to know what mini-pronouns can be used for. Mini pronouns are only used to "shrink" ğŸª„ or replace **two things**:

1. The **servant** <ServantIcon /> of a <VP />
3. A **possesive**

### 1. As a shrunken servant

<div className="alert alert-info" role="alert">

What's a servant?

There's an explanation about what a <strong>servant</strong> <ServantIcon /> is in the section on <Link to="/phrase-structure/vp/#2-with-non-past-transitive-verbs-">VPs</Link>. Basically a servant is either:

- the **object** <NP /> of a non-past transitive verb
- the **subject** <NP /> of a past-tense transitive verb

We call these the **servant** <ServantIcon /> of the <VP /> because they're not the **king** <KingIcon /> of the phrase -- they don't control the verb. (There is no servant with an intransitive verb.)

</div>

The "servant" <NP /> in a <VP /> can be "shrunken" ğŸª„ and replaced with a mini pronoun.

## Other Notes

### Don't confuse with other <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "Ø¯Û", f: "de" }} />'s

The mini-pronoun <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "Ø¯Û", f: "de" }} /> is not to be confused with two other particles that look and sound the exact same:

 - The jussive <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "Ø¯Û", f: "de", e: "should, must" }} />
 - The demonstrative pronoun <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "Ø¯Û", f: "de", e: "this" }} />
 - The inflected form of the 3rd pers fem near pronoun <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "Ø¯Ø§", f: "daa", e: "she" }} /> (<InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "Ø¯Û", f: "de" }} />)
