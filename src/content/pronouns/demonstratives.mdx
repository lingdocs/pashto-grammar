## The general Ø¯Ø§ - daa

The word <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "Ø¯Ø§", f: "daa", e: "this / these" }} /> is probably the most common demonstrative in Pashto. It's also fairly general, for pointing to people or things close by or at a medium distance. ("Distance" here can refer to the distance in conversation, not just physical distance.)

The other nice thing about this useful pointer word is that **it is the same for masculine or feminine, singular or plural**!

### Inflecting Ø¯Ø§ - daa to Ø¯Û - de

In all these examples above, masculine and feminine, singular or plural, we always use <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "Ø¯Ø§", f: "daa" }} />. But what happens when we need to <Link to="/inflection/inflection-intro/">inflect</Link> a word because it's <Link to="/sandwiches/sandwiches/">in a sandwich</Link> or because it's <Link to="/verbs/past-verbs/#past-tense-with-transitive-verbs-">the subject of a past tense transitive verb</Link>? In that case it becomes <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "Ø¯Û", f: "de" }} />.

Remember, <Link to="/inflection/inflection-intro/">normally</Link> we would inflect a word when it's plural, but not with <InlinePs opts={opts} ps={{ p: "Ø¯Ø§", f: "daa" }} />! **we only inflect this word for _two_ reasons**:

- being in a sandwich
- being the subject of a past tense transitive verb
